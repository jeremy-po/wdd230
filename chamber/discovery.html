Demographics

Arima, officially known as The Royal Chartered Borough of Arima, has a population of 33,606 residents as per the 2011 census, with an urban population reaching 65,623. It is the fourth-largest municipality in Trinidad and Tobago, following Port of Spain, Chaguanas, and San Fernando. The borough exhibits a high population density of 2,801 people per square kilometer (7,250 per square mile). Situated at the south central foothills of the Northern Range, Arima is geographically adjacent to Sangre Grande and Arouca and lies south of the Caroni–Arena Dam. This borough, settled in 1757 and achieving borough status on August 1, 1888, has grown into a significant urban center within the country.
